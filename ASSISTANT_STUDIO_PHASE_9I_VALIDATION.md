# Assistant's Studio — Phase 9I Validation

Purpose: verify that Fast and Full are now visibly different after Phases 9D–9H.

## Validation goals

Fast should be shorter but still actionable.

Full should be noticeably denser and more detailed.

Full should include more timing, contingency, backstage, and coordination detail.

Neither mode should read like a summary.

## Quick test procedure

Use the same scenario in both Fast and Full.

Recommended scenarios:
- Levitation Illusion
- Audience Prediction
- Motorcycle Appearance
- Walkaround Card Routine

For each scenario:
1. Run Fast mode.
2. Run Full mode.
3. Compare section count, bullet count, detail density, and wording.

## Pass / fail checklist

### Fast mode passes if:
- output feels like a quick rehearsal planning sheet
- content is shorter than Full at a glance
- bullets describe concrete assistant actions
- wording sounds operational, not reflective or advisory
- output stays roughly within 4 sections and 3–4 bullets per section

### Full mode passes if:
- output is clearly longer and denser than Fast
- bullets include timing coordination, volunteer handling, backstage prep, resets, and contingencies
- wording reads like a professional assistant operations document
- output stays roughly within 6 sections and 4–5 bullets per section

### Both modes fail if:
- bullets read like generic summaries
- bullets discuss what assistants "should consider" instead of what they physically do
- Fast and Full look nearly identical
- Full does not show more detailed timing, coordination, or contingency planning than Fast

## Recommended test prompts

### Test 1 — Stage illusion
Routine: Motorcycle appearance in a mid-size theater with two assistants, one volunteer, limited wing space, and a 90-second reset.

### Test 2 — Mentalism / prediction
Routine: Audience prediction routine in a corporate ballroom with one assistant, one volunteer, and a reveal envelope on a side table.

### Test 3 — Walkaround
Routine: Walkaround card routine for a banquet with one assistant managing props, crowd flow, and reset between tables.

## What to look for in Full that should be stronger than Fast
- clearer cue timing
- more precise volunteer positioning
- backstage prep details
- contingency actions if a volunteer hesitates or misses a mark
- reset sequencing after applause or reveal

## Suggested visual review note

At a glance, Full should look substantially more substantial than Fast before reading line by line.
